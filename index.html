Research Watch

Highlighted ongoing directions

These short research-watch notes capture themes I am actively tracking beyond the static section pages: emerging attack surfaces, new defense directions, and system-level gaps becoming important in real AI deployment.

Research Watch

Why agentic AI changes the security problem

Once AI systems can plan, remember, call tools, and act over time, security is no longer only about model outputs. The challenge becomes how to bound autonomy, protect memory, and prevent prompt-level manipulation from turning into real actions.

Research Watch

Edge AI makes implementation details matter

On-device deployment changes the attacker model completely. Secure boot, firmware trust, model extraction, side-channel leakage, sensor validation, and update integrity all become part of whether the AI system is actually trustworthy.

Research Watch

Physical AI needs security and safety together

In embodied and real-time systems, perception errors, communication delay, control instability, and cyber attacks cannot be studied separately. The real challenge is securing the full perception-to-action loop under realistic operating conditions.
